Rules:

Players: 2/4. Calculator required for keeping track of the points.

All players start at 10,000 life points. Move through the board by rolling 2 dices.

Land on the various colored tiles to draw a card associated with the tiles' colors. Land on Send to HELL & the player will be sent to HELL space, where they can choose to stay there & lose 500 life points for every moving players' turns, OR get out of HELL by inserting themselves into other players' turns & disrupt the turn order at the cost of half of their points. For SAFE SPACE, passing through it will give the player 1,000 points, while landing on it gives 2,000.

To win, get 20,000 points & finish the lap (by either lands or passes through START), OR finish 3 laps before other players do so, OR be the last person standing.

If a player's points are 0 or getting into the negatives, they will only be able to roll 1 dice at a time. Also, landing or passing through START will cause said player to be eliminated for the rest of the game.

MDA Analysis:

The mechanics of this board game, at its core, focuses on number calculation, where numbers fluctuate depending on various factors such as what cards are at play & what spaces the players passed or landed. Numbers aside, there's also a condition where a player who managed to finish 3 laps first would win. This would involve lucky dice rolls that resulted in having said player moves many spaces while being able to avoid tiles that would negatively affect them, both in movement & in points.

To spice up that mechanics, the added dynamics include ability to screw up the turn order by being inside a jail space equivalent. Switching up the turn order may or may not give the losing player an advantage over the upcoming winner, but at least they will be able to interrupt other players indirectly as certain in-game cards are stated to be affected by players' turn orders. The tile space numbers are also reduced so that there's no empty spaces where nothing happens, as well as to speed up the game's pace.

As for the aesthetics, I want the board game to be an edutainment of sorts; the cards in the game include pictures & descriptions of interesting facts, myths, & trivias related to my home country Thailand & their cultures. This aesthetics is chosen because Thai cultures are something worth preserving, yet people in the country keeps on incorporating modern western cultures while have their own traditions neglected. With this kind of aesthetics, I at least hope that people playing the board game will be interested in Thai cultures & some fun facts & trivias related to them.
